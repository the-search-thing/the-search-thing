the-search-thing

hi everyone, we are building the-search-thing because search should be fast and accurate

Semantically search files, images, and videos on your machine—local-first, keyboard-friendly, still very early.

what it does

features

A local-first search layer for your OS—semantic, fast, and a little whimsical while it works.

  • Semantic search

    Describe what you need in plain language. the-search-thing matches meaning across filenames, bodies, and media—not just keywords.

    Text, images, and videos are embedded so “that diagram from the investor deck” can surface without remembering the exact path.

  • Built for sub-millisecond feel

    Interactive search is tuned for instant feedback. Results should land while you are still typing the thought.

    We keep pushing indexing and query paths until the app feels as fast as muscle memory—not “fast for Electron.”

  • Local-first by default

    Your index lives on your machine. Nothing is uploaded to our servers today—you stay in control of your files.

    When optional hosting exists later, the plan is encrypted storage. Until then: fully local, fully yours.

  • Smart directory indexing

    Point at a folder once. Ignore rules skip noise; jobs run in the background with clear progress per media type.

    Videos are chunked with transcripts and frame summaries. Images get structured summaries before embedding. Text scans and indexes in batches.

  • Keyboard-native workflow

    Global shortcuts to summon search, kick off indexing, and open settings—designed for people who live in the launcher.

    Rebind keys to taste. Search history remembers recent queries so you can jump back without retyping.

  • One bar, many file types

    Filter to files, folders, or both. File-type icons and previews help you recognize hits before you open them.

    Supported types and ignore lists are configurable—tune what gets indexed so search stays relevant.

questions

faq

Straight answers about search, privacy, and why it is still Electron—for now.

  • how did you build search?

    We index directories on your machine—text, images, and videos—then embed content so you can query by meaning. Ignore rules and file-type config keep the index focused.

  • is my data safe?

    Yes. We are not hosting your files today; everything runs locally. If we add optional cloud features later, the plan is encrypted storage end to end.

  • is it fast?

    That is the whole point. Interactive search targets sub-millisecond feel; indexing runs in the background so you can keep working while folders are processed.

  • what can I search?

    Files, folders, images, and videos from folders you choose. Video indexing uses transcripts and frame summaries; images get structured summaries before embedding.

  • ew but this is electron!

    We know. We shipped something we wanted to use quickly. A native GPUI path (Zed's framework) is on the roadmap when the core search experience is solid.
